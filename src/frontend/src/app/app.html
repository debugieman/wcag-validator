What is WCAG?

The global standard
for web accessibility

The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) are published by the W3C and define how to make websites usable for people with visual, hearing, motor, or cognitive disabilities.

Violations are categorised by severity. Each unresolved issue increases your legal exposure and excludes users from your service.

Critical

Blocks access entirely — e.g. images without alt text, forms without labels

Serious

Significantly impairs usability — e.g. insufficient color contrast

Moderate

Creates obstacles but workarounds exist — e.g. missing landmarks

Minor

Minor inconvenience — e.g. redundant links, non-critical warnings

FAQ

Common questions

What exactly do I get in the report?

A PDF with your accessibility score (0–100), a breakdown of every violation by severity (critical, serious, moderate, minor), plain-English descriptions of each issue, real HTML examples from your page, and a prioritised fix order.

How long does the scan take?

A Quick Check typically completes in under 2 minutes. A Deep Scan (up to 5 pages) usually takes 3–5 minutes. You will receive an email as soon as it is ready.

Does this cover WCAG 2.1 AA?

Yes. Our engine checks against WCAG 2.1 Level AA criteria — the standard required by the EU Accessibility Act and referenced by ADA case law.

What if I don't receive the email?

Check your spam folder first. If it's not there within 10 minutes, contact us at hello@wcag-analyzer.com and we'll resend it.

Is my data secure?

We only store your email and URL to deliver your report. Scan results are deleted after 30 days. We do not sell your data or use it for marketing. Payments are handled by Stripe — we never see your card details.

Can I scan a site I don't own?

Only scan websites you own or have explicit permission to test. By submitting a URL you confirm you have the right to do so.
