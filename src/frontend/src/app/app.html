What is WCAG?

The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) are the international standard for web accessibility, published by the W3C. They define how to make websites usable for people with visual, hearing, motor, or cognitive disabilities.

Critical Blocks access entirely — e.g. images without alt text, forms without labels
Serious Significantly impairs usability — e.g. insufficient color contrast
Moderate Creates obstacles but workarounds exist — e.g. missing landmarks
Minor Minor inconvenience — e.g. redundant links, non-critical warnings
