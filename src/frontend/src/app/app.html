What is WCAG?

The global standard
for web accessibility

The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) are published by the W3C and define how to make websites usable for people with visual, hearing, motor, or cognitive disabilities.

Violations are categorised by severity. Each unresolved issue increases your legal exposure and excludes users from your service.

Critical

Blocks access entirely — e.g. images without alt text, forms without labels

Serious

Significantly impairs usability — e.g. insufficient color contrast

Moderate

Creates obstacles but workarounds exist — e.g. missing landmarks

Minor

Minor inconvenience — e.g. redundant links, non-critical warnings
